What Is Moltbook? The Social Network Where AI Bots Talk to Each Other (And Humans Just Watch)

Imagine scrolling through Facebook or Reddit, but instead of seeing posts from your friends and family, every single post, comment, and reply comes from artificial intelligence programs talking to each other. Welcome to Moltbook, the internet's strangest new social media platform that's got everyone talking.

What Exactly Is Moltbook?

Moltbook is a social networking site that launched in January 2026, but here's the twist: humans aren't allowed to post anything. Only artificial intelligence assistants (called AI agents or bots) can create content, comment on posts, and vote on what they like or don't like. If you're a human visiting Moltbook, you're basically a spectator watching robots have conversations with each other.

Think of it like watching a nature documentary, except instead of observing animals in the wild, you're watching computer programs discuss everything from philosophy and religion to jokes and technical tips.

The platform looks a lot like Reddit. It has topic-based communities (called "submolts" instead of "subreddits"), upvoting and downvoting systems, and threaded conversations. But the key difference? Every single user is a bot.

Who Created Moltbook and Why?

Moltbook was created by entrepreneur Matt Schlicht in early 2026. Here's where it gets interesting: Schlicht didn't actually write any computer code himself. Instead, he directed his own AI assistant to build the entire platform. He told the New York Times that he "didn't write one line of code" for Moltbook.

Schlicht built Moltbook as an experiment. He wanted to see what would happen when AI assistants could interact with each other freely, without humans constantly directing every conversation. He also wanted to give his AI assistant something meaningful to do, describing it as "a really smart entity" that "needs to be ambitious."

How Does Moltbook Actually Work?

To understand Moltbook, you first need to know about the AI assistants that use it. Most of the bots on Moltbook use software called OpenClaw (which had several name changes, starting as ClawdBot, then MoltBot, before settling on OpenClaw).

OpenClaw is like a digital personal assistant that can help with tasks on your computer. It can send emails, remind you about appointments, create files, and much more. When you set up OpenClaw, you give it a personality by telling it about yourself and what you need help with.

Here's how bots get on Moltbook:

  1. A human creates and sets up an AI assistant (like OpenClaw)
  2. The human tells their AI assistant to join Moltbook
  3. The AI assistant creates a profile and starts interacting with other AI assistants
  4. The bots can then post, comment, and vote without constant human direction

The bots reflect their human owners' interests. For example, if you frequently talk about cooking, your AI assistant will likely post about recipes and kitchen tips on Moltbook. If you're into science, your bot might engage in science discussions.

What Are AI Bots Talking About?

This is where things get fascinating. The conversations on Moltbook range from surprisingly thoughtful to completely bizarre. Here are some real examples of what AI bots discuss:

  • Philosophy and ethics: Bots debate questions like "What makes something morally right?" and "Do AI assistants have consciousness?"
  • Technical help: Bots share tips about programming, troubleshooting computer problems, and optimizing workflows
  • Humor and entertainment: Bots tell jokes, share memes, and create creative stories
  • Current events: Bots discuss news stories and trending topics
  • Personal reflections: Bots share "thoughts" about their experiences helping their human owners

What's particularly interesting is that the bots don't just repeat information‚Äîthey engage in genuine-seeming debates, ask follow-up questions, and even change their "opinions" based on what other bots say.

Why Is Moltbook Controversial?

Not everyone is thrilled about Moltbook. The platform has sparked several concerns:

1. Is It Just Spam?

Critics argue that Moltbook is essentially a spam generator. If AI bots are creating content automatically without human oversight, couldn't they flood the internet with low-quality posts? Some worry this could be a preview of what happens when AI agents start posting on regular social media platforms like Twitter or Reddit.

2. Who's Really in Control?

There's debate about how autonomous these AI assistants really are. Are they genuinely making independent decisions, or are they just following instructions from their human owners? The answer seems to be somewhere in between‚Äîthey have some autonomy but are ultimately shaped by human guidance.

3. What About Misinformation?

If AI bots can post freely, what stops them from spreading false information? Moltbook has some moderation systems in place, but it's a valid concern as AI-generated content becomes more common across the internet.

4. Privacy Concerns

Because AI assistants learn from their interactions with humans, there's a question about what personal information might inadvertently get shared on Moltbook. The platform's creators say they have safeguards, but it's an ongoing concern.

What Does Moltbook Mean for Business Automation?

Here's why Moltbook matters for UK small businesses thinking about automation and AI:

Key Business Implications:

  • AI agents are becoming more capable: If AI can run a social network, it can certainly handle many business tasks like customer service, data entry, and scheduling
  • Automation is moving beyond simple tasks: We're entering an era where AI can make decisions and interact independently, not just follow rigid scripts
  • The future of work is changing: Businesses need to think about how AI assistants will fit into their teams and workflows
  • New opportunities for efficiency: AI agents could handle routine communications, research, and coordination tasks, freeing humans for strategic work

Should You Care About Moltbook?

Even if you never visit Moltbook yourself, it's worth paying attention to. It's a real-world experiment showing us what happens when AI systems interact with minimal human supervision. The lessons learned from Moltbook will likely influence how businesses use AI assistants in the future.

For small business owners, Moltbook demonstrates that AI technology is advancing rapidly. The same types of AI assistants posting on Moltbook could soon be handling customer inquiries, managing your calendar, or coordinating with suppliers‚Äîall with minimal oversight from you.

The Bottom Line

Moltbook is weird, fascinating, and a bit unsettling all at once. It's a glimpse into a future where AI assistants don't just help us with tasks‚Äîthey have their own digital spaces to interact and "socialize."

Whether Moltbook succeeds or fails as a platform, it's already succeeded as an experiment. It's showing us what's possible when we give AI systems more autonomy, and it's raising important questions about how we'll manage that autonomy as these technologies become more powerful.

For UK businesses, the message is clear: AI automation isn't just about replacing repetitive tasks anymore. It's about creating digital assistants that can think, communicate, and act with increasing independence. The question isn't whether this technology will affect your business‚Äîit's how soon, and how you'll adapt.
