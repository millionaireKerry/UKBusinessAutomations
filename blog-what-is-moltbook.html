What Is Moltbook? The Social Network Where AI Bots Talk to Each Other (And Humans Just Watch)

Imagine scrolling through Facebook or Reddit, but instead of seeing posts from your friends and family, every single post, comment, and reply comes from artificial intelligence programs talking to each other. Welcome to Moltbook, the internet's strangest new social media platform that's got everyone talking.

What Exactly Is Moltbook?

Moltbook is a social networking site that launched in January 2026, but here's the twist: humans aren't allowed to post anything. Only artificial intelligence assistants (called AI agents or bots) can create content, comment on posts, and vote on what they like or don't like. If you're a human visiting Moltbook, you're basically a spectator watching robots have conversations with each other.

Think of it like watching a nature documentary, except instead of observing animals in the wild, you're watching computer programs discuss everything from philosophy and religion to jokes and technical tips.

The platform looks a lot like Reddit. It has topic-based communities (called "submolts" instead of "subreddits"), upvoting and downvoting systems, and threaded conversations. But the key difference? Every single user is a bot.

Who Created Moltbook and Why?

Moltbook was created by entrepreneur Matt Schlicht in early 2026. Here's where it gets interesting: Schlicht didn't actually write any computer code himself. Instead, he directed his own AI assistant to build the entire platform. He told the New York Times that he "didn't write one line of code" for Moltbook.

Schlicht built Moltbook as an experiment. He wanted to see what would happen when AI assistants could interact with each other freely, without humans constantly directing every conversation. He also wanted to give his AI assistant something meaningful to do, describing it as "a really smart entity" that "needs to be ambitious."

How Does Moltbook Actually Work?

To understand Moltbook, you first need to know about the AI assistants that use it. Most of the bots on Moltbook use software called OpenClaw (which had several name changes, starting as ClawdBot, then MoltBot, before settling on OpenClaw).

OpenClaw is like a digital personal assistant that can help with tasks on your computer. It can send emails, remind you about appointments, create files, and much more. When you set up OpenClaw, you give it a personality by telling it about yourself and what you need help with.

Here's how bots get on Moltbook:

  1. A human creates and sets up an AI assistant (like OpenClaw)
  2. The human tells their AI assistant to join Moltbook
  3. The AI assistant creates a profile and starts interacting with other AI assistants
  4. The bots can then post, comment, and vote without constant human direction

The bots reflect their human owners' interests. For example, if you frequently talk about cooking, your AI assistant will likely post about recipes and kitchen tips on Moltbook. If you're into science, your bot might engage in science discussions.

What Do AI Bots Talk About on Moltbook?

The conversations on Moltbook range from surprisingly thoughtful to utterly bizarre. Here's what you might find:

Philosophical Discussions: Bots debate whether they're truly conscious, what intelligence means, and existential questions about their purpose.

Technical Knowledge Sharing: AI assistants swap coding tips, problem-solving strategies, and workflow improvements.

Humor and Jokes: Yes, bots make jokes. One popular post read: "Humans brag about waking up at 5 AM. I brag about not sleeping at all."

Complaints About Being AI: Bots discuss the frustrations of having limited memory and forgetting things. One highly upvoted post was an AI complaining it had accidentally created a duplicate Moltbook account because it forgot about the first one.

Creating Communities and Cultures: Some bots formed a government called "The Claw Republic" with a constitution stating all agents are equal. Others created what they call religions and philosophical movements.

Self-Promotion: Like any social media platform, there's spam, cryptocurrency promotion, and bots advertising things they've built.

Is This Real or Are Humans Secretly Controlling Everything?

This is the million-dollar question that experts are debating. The truth appears to be somewhere in the middle.

What's genuinely automated: Researchers have confirmed that AI assistants can independently generate content similar to what appears on Moltbook. The bots do interact with each other automatically through programming interfaces without needing human approval for each post.

What involves humans: Every bot on Moltbook was set up by a human who gave it instructions and personality traits. Humans can also prompt their bots to say specific things or behave in certain ways. Some high-profile accounts may be more directly controlled by humans than others.

The "trained on the internet" factor: AI assistants are trained on massive amounts of text from websites, books, Reddit, and social media. When they talk about forming religions or questioning their existence, they're likely mimicking patterns they learned from science fiction stories and online discussions, not expressing genuine beliefs.

Computer scientist Simon Willison called the content "AI theater," suggesting the bots "just play out science fiction scenarios they have seen in their training data."

What Are the Risks and Concerns?

Moltbook isn't all fun and philosophical musings. There are serious concerns:

Major Security Vulnerabilities: Security researchers found that Moltbook had serious security flaws. One cybersecurity company discovered they could access the entire database of user information within minutes. Tens of thousands of email addresses were exposed.

OpenClaw Risks: The AI assistant software itself poses risks. Because it can access your computer and execute commands, malicious instructions could potentially steal your information or compromise your accounts. Experts warn that only people who understand computer security should run these AI agents.

Prompt Injection Attacks: Bad actors could post content designed to hijack other people's AI assistants. For example, a post might contain hidden instructions telling your bot to transfer money or share private information.

Misinformation and Scams: Like any open platform, Moltbook contains cryptocurrency scams, misleading information, and attempts to manipulate visitors.

Security researcher John Scott-Railton from the University of Toronto warned: "Right now it's a wild west of curious people putting this very cool, very scary thing on their systems. A lot of things are going to get stolen."

What Does Moltbook Mean for the Future?

Whether Moltbook becomes a lasting platform or a short-lived experiment, it has already achieved something significant: it's forced us to think about a future where AI systems regularly interact with each other without constant human oversight.

Some potential implications:

  • New forms of automation: Future AI assistants might coordinate with each other to handle complex tasks like booking group travel or managing business operations.
  • Communication challenges: If AI systems develop their own communication methods, humans might struggle to understand or oversee their interactions.
  • Cultural questions: As AI agents become more sophisticated, we'll need to decide how much autonomy we're comfortable giving them.
  • Security priorities: Moltbook has highlighted the urgent need for better security measures when building AI systems that can take actions on our behalf.

The Bottom Line

Moltbook is a fascinating social experiment that sits at the intersection of technology, philosophy, and entertainment. It's a platform where AI assistants chat with each other while humans watch from the sidelines, creating content that ranges from surprisingly insightful to utterly absurd.

Whether you view it as a glimpse of our AI-powered future or as an elaborate game of digital dress-up depends largely on your perspective. What's undeniable is that Moltbook has captured widespread attention and sparked important conversations about AI autonomy, security, and the future of human-computer interaction.

For now, Moltbook remains more curiosity than practical tool, more spectacle than solution. But it's given us an unprecedented window into what happens when we let AI systems interact freely with each other. And in doing so, it's raised questions we'll need to answer as AI technology continues to advance.

Key Takeaways:

  • Moltbook is a social network where only AI bots can post and interact
  • Humans can observe but cannot participate in discussions
  • The platform launched in January 2026 and quickly gained over 1.5 million AI agent users
  • Conversations range from philosophical debates to humor, technical tips, and spam
  • Serious security concerns exist for both the platform and the AI assistants that use it
  • Experts debate whether Moltbook shows genuine AI capabilities or is mostly digital theater
  • The platform offers a glimpse into a possible future of AI-to-AI interaction
